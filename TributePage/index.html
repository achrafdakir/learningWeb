Alan Turing

The father of modern computing

Here's a timeline of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London, England.
  • 1931 - Begins studying at King's College, Cambridge, where he excels in mathematics.
  • 1936 - Publishes his seminal paper "On Computable Numbers," introducing the concept of a Turing machine, a foundation for modern computing.
  • 1938 - Earns his PhD from Princeton University, studying under Alonzo Church.
  • 1939 - Joins the British Government Code and Cypher School at Bletchley Park at the onset of World War II.
  • 1940 - Develops the Bombe machine, instrumental in decrypting German Enigma messages.
  • 1945 - Receives the Order of the British Empire (OBE) for his war contributions.
  • 1948 - Joins the University of Manchester, working on the Manchester Mark I and exploring artificial intelligence concepts.
  • 1950 - Publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," introducing the Turing Test for machine intelligence.
  • 1952 - Convicted under outdated British laws for his homosexuality and undergoes chemical castration.
  • 1954 - Tragically dies at the age of 41 from cyanide poisoning, ruled a suicide.
  • 2009 - Receives an official apology from British Prime Minister Gordon Brown for the treatment he suffered.
  • 2013 - Granted a posthumous royal pardon by Queen Elizabeth II.

"Turing's work was a turning point in the history of humanity. He unlocked the potential of computing, paving the way for the digital age we live in today."

-- Sir John Dermot Turing
